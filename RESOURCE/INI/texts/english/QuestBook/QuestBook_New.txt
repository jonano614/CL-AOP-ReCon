Recipe.cartridge
{
Paper Cartridge
***************
A remarkable innovation of the Swedish musketeer regiments, allowing loading to be twice as fast!

To make one cartridge, you’ll also need one bullet and one measure of powder.
Take a piece of paper about half the size of your palm, roll it into a tube and pour in the powder, then place the bullet on top.
Make the cartridge so that it fits freely into the barrel.
Before loading, don’t forget to bite off the bottom of the cartridge to access the powder.

Components: Granulated powder, Lead bullet.
}
Recipe.petard
{
Petard
*******
A flashbang device that can temporarily disorient enemy personnel without damaging their material assets.

Take a thin sheet of bronze, a mold shaped like a gun chamber, and press the sheet over the mold to form a cup.
Carefully pour in ten charges of gunpowder, tightly rivet the bronze casing, and insert it into the barrel.
Use an awl or punch to make a priming hole, then plug it with soft wax. Store the fuse separately!

Components: Bronze pot, Granulated gunpowder, Candle, Rope.
Tools: Locksmith’s tools.
}
Recipe.grenade
{
Grenade
*******
A Spanish invention perfected by the British, capable of striking significant enemy forces at a considerable distance.

Prepare a bronze sheet and about a dozen bullets.
Shape the bronze sheet over a mold to match the size of the mortar barrel, and lay the bullets at the bottom, one next to another.
Pour in up to ten charges of gunpowder, place the remaining bullets on top, and seal the casing.
Drill a priming hole in the lower part, and plug it with a wooden spool.
In battle, on command, remove the spool, insert a fuse or a match cord soaked in saltpeter, about a finger’s length or so, but no longer than the chamber, and secure it with wax or the same spool.

Components: Bronze pot, Granulated gunpowder, Lead bullets, Candle, Rope.
Tools: Locksmith’s tools.
}

Recipe.GunEchin
{
Hedgehog Shot
************
Slugs, also known as chopped pieces of iron rod, can be used against enemy personnel, though effectively only at close range, where their impact is truly devastating.
However, they are light and the force of the gunpowder tends to scatter them away from the target, so aiming does not require much precision.
Secondly, they are hard and scratch the inside of the gun barrel, making them unsuitable for fine or long barrels.
Some prefer slugs over buckshot, claiming they hit harder and cause more severe wounds, as the angular shape of the slug tears the body more on entry, resulting in a painful, ragged, bloody wound—more terrifying for being shallow but gaping.

To make a single shot, simply chop up nails or similar iron scraps and bind the pieces together with wax for easier loading and to give the shot a tighter spread.
The resulting cylinder is easier and more practical to tie together with the powder charge in a paper cartridge.

Components: Nails, Candle, Granulated powder.
Tools: Metalworking tools, Crucible, Oil lamp.
}

Recipe.cartridge_colt
{
Revolver Cartridge
*********************
Used for compact storage of the powder charge and bullet needed for firing, as well as for speeding up handling and making it easier to load revolvers with a non-removable cylinder.
Take the thinnest paper you can find and thoroughly rub it with a mixture of gunpowder, alcohol, and wax.
Let it dry.
Coat the base of the bullet with paste and form a case from the paper in the shape of a truncated cylinder.
Pour black powder into the case and tamp it down.
Fold the free end of the paper, apply glue, and bend it to either side so that the glued side is on the side, and the end has a smooth, clean layer of paper.
Wrap the finished cartridge in a piece of parchment or leather for protection.
When loading, pierce the paper base for reliability.

Components: Revolver Bullet, Revolver Powder.
Tools: Tailor’s Kit, Candle.
}
Recipe.GunCap_colt
{
Caps
*******
Single-use, fitted onto the percussion tubes one at a time. Used to ignite the charge in a revolver’s chamber.
They work perfectly in any weather and at any angle.

To make them, you’ll need a sheet of pure bronze no thicker than a bay leaf, a chisel, a crucible, and a vise.
Cut neat circles from the sheet, each with a diameter three times the height of the percussion tube. Remove 4–5 sectors around the edge to ensure the cap fits evenly onto the tube.
Carefully melt wax, add fulminating silver, and place the mixture into the resulting cylinder.

Components: Bronze Pot, Candles, Fulminating Silver.
Tools: Crucible, Lamp, Locksmith’s Tools.
}
Recipe.fulminate_silver
{
Fulminating Silver
****************
An explosive substance used to ignite gunpowder in a cartridge.

Place a charge of 80 grains of the purest metallic silver you can obtain into a flask with a capacity of 1/5 pint, and dissolve it in 1/10 pint of nitric acid. Cover the neck of the flask to prevent the escape of brown gases. Wait until the silver is completely dissolved. If a white precipitate forms, add a spoonful of water and heat gently until it disappears.
In a flask with a capacity of one pint, place 1/9 pint of alcohol. Pour in the prepared solution of silver in nitric acid and heat the flask. The liquid will begin to boil and foam. Add a little more alcohol. Soon, white, shiny crystals will settle at the bottom. After this, cool the flask by placing it in water.
When the bubbling subsides and the solution cools, separate the crystals from the solution. Avoid shocks and friction with the crystals—it is dangerous! Rinse with cold water and dry.
Store and transport with the utmost care. Do not use larger quantities of silver, acid, or alcohol at one time than specified in the recipe, to avoid explosion.

Components: Silver Ingot, Nitric Acid, Alcohol.
Tools: Alchemy Set, Lamp.
}

Recipe.potion1
{
Healing Potion
****************
An infusion of medicinal herbs that heals minor wounds.

Grind 1 ounce of matricaria flowers and dried ginger root into a fine powder using a mortar and pestle.
Take a bunch of white grapes weighing about 5-6 ounces, crush the berries into a pulp, and mix with the herbal powder until a thick, uniform suspension forms. Place the resulting mixture into a clean flask.
Let the mixture steep just until the powder settles completely at the bottom, then carefully strain the clear fraction of the elixir through a clean cloth.

Components: Matricaria, Ginger Root, Grapes.
Tools: Mortar and pestle.
}

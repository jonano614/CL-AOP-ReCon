... from Caracas we sailed along the coast to an Indian settlement that harvested copra for me personally. Being a friend of the Indians is very profitable. I will buy copra from them, bypassing all the trade markups of resellers...
... that day there was a celebration in the settlement. The high chief of a neighboring tribe was visiting with his family and clan leaders. Countless amounts of casiri were drunk. There I saw her - the embodiment of natural beauty and tenderness itself. I realized that she was the one destined by fate to become my wife...
... her name was Wawaya, and she turned out to be the youngest daughter of the high chief of a neighboring tribe. I met her and gave the first gifts to win her heart immediately. And it seems I succeeded - my courtship was received favorably...
... I am staying for a few more days. Copra is being loaded onto the ship, and I continue to communicate with young Wawaya. During this time, I fell in love with her completely and irrevocably. Thank the Creator, she developed reciprocal feelings for me! I am beside myself with happiness! According to tribal customs, I am preparing a bride price for her father...
... he refused me! Wawaya's father did not accept my generous offerings and literally threw me out of his hut. I saw tears on my beloved's cheeks - she also wants to be with me! So let what must happen, happen! I will not allow some savage, even if he is her father, to destroy our happiness...
... Wawaya insists on sacrificing several precious jewelry to her tribe's gods before escaping, to secure their support. I did not object - when we arrive in New Spain, my beloved will gradually wean herself from pagan customs, accept baptism, and become a respectable Christian woman. For now, we are heading to an idol deep in the jungle...
... a white man could never find this sacred place on his own. Along the way, I could have died a hundred times if not for Wawaya - she showed me hidden pit traps with sharp stakes on the paths, scattered poisoned thorns, and set crossbows...
... it's something incredible! I have never seen so much gold! There were nuggets the size of goose eggs! Even half of these riches would be enough to provide for myself for the rest of my days. Wawaya objected, but I managed to persuade her. I even doubled the size of the donation to appease my beloved...
... only two weeks later did I, my first mate, and quartermaster deliver the Indian gold to the coast in a covered wagon. No one else on the ship knows about it - I don't trust the sailors. Moreover, the crew is irritated by the long idle time off the wild shores where I took the 'Santa Lucia' to avoid pursuit by Wawaya's father...
... at night, the three of us transferred the gold to the ship and hid it in one of the crates, throwing excess copra overboard. We covered the gold with a thick layer of copra - none of the sailors would ever guess the contents of this inconspicuous crate. Finally, we are setting sail. Just a couple of weeks' journey - and my sweet Wawaya and I will reach New Spain and marry in church. We are made for each other and will be together until death do us part...
... the calm has been going on for the third day. The 'Santa Lucia' is barely moving, and we haven't even reached the latitude of Jamaica yet. I see the sidelong glances the ignorant sailors give my beautiful Wawaya - they consider her presence on the ship a bad omen...
... today, one of the sailors, laying out rat poison in the hold, dropped a vial of arsenic right into the barrel of drinking water. How he managed it is still unclear to me. Fifty gallons of fresh water are spoiled. The dead calm continues. My beloved is praying to her gods for forgiveness - she sincerely believes this is punishment for the stolen gold...
... already the tenth man has fallen, struck by a suddenly outbreak of fever. The sailors are grumbling. The boatswain can barely maintain discipline. This evening, the body of the first to die from the illness was buried at sea. Fresh water is starting to run short. God, send us wind!
... finally, a fresh breeze filled our sails. But there is little joy - a third of the crew lies in delirious fever. Tonight, several sailors stole a crate of expensive rum from the hold and drank it. The boatswain severely punished them with the whip and sent them to the brig. The crew looks at me and the officers like wolves, but my poor Wawaya gets the worst of it. I don't let her on deck, as I fear for her life. If only we could reach Cuba - I will make a stop there, fill the barrels with water, and recruit a new crew...
... the cursed apostates appeared as if from nowhere. Their schooner, if my crew weren't in such a pitiful state, wouldn't pose a serious threat to the 'Santa Lucia' with her twenty-pound guns. Sailors, weakened by fever, literally fell from the shrouds into the water. The pirates' cannonballs pierced the sides and deck in several places, but did not cause critical damage. Gathering all my will and calling on the Almighty for help, we fought off the scoundrels, forcing them to retreat...
... the crew demands Wawaya's death. They consider the poor Indian girl a witch and want to get rid of her. They attribute all the troubles and misfortunes that have befallen us to her appearance. I and my loyal officers will defend Wawaya to the last. It seems a mutiny is brewing, and the shores of Cuba are still not visible - apparently, after the battle with the pirates, we veered off course...
... the deck is covered in blood. The first mate and boatswain are dead, the quartermaster is still alive, judging by the sounds of his musket shots, but he won't last long against a crowd of enraged sailors. I have barricaded myself in the cabin and am putting up successful resistance, firing back with a musket. My beloved stands behind me and hands me charges. Her sweet face is contorted with fear. But she is not afraid of the mutineers. Looking into my eyes, she whispers: 'The gods are taking revenge!'...
... my heart is breaking with grief! A pistol bullet that pierced the cabin door hit my beloved Wawaya right in her beautiful breast. I am trying to stop the bleeding, but nothing works - I don't have the skills of a surgeon... Only my musket is holding the besiegers back from a direct assault on the locked door. Ammunition is running low...
... poor Wawaya has died. She died peacefully, with a smile on her lips, accepting death without a word of reproach. There was no fear in her eyes anymore - only love and sadness were reflected in them. I said a prayer and prepare myself to meet the Almighty - without my beloved, life has lost its meaning... A bullet that flew in has pierced my leg. I can barely move...
... the gunpowder has run out. I have only four charges left in a pair of double-barreled pistols. I sit down at the table and prepare to meet my death, as befits a white man and a ship's captain. Through the cabin window, I see a storm approaching on the horizon. It will be very strong...
... they are breaking down the door. In a few minutes, it will all be over, and I will meet my sweet Wawaya again in a better world. I am writing these lines thinking of her. Was she right, and is all this punishment for sacrilege? The chain of events that led to the tragedy looks like a curse. 'The gods are taking revenge'! The crate with gold... who will it belong to after this...

P.
